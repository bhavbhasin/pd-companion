Food is one of the most underappreciated variables in Parkinson's.

Timing meals around levodopa matters more than most people realize - dietary protein competes with the medication for the same absorption pathway, so a meal at the wrong moment can blunt a dose. It's well documented, and almost impossible to see in the moment.

So we built a way to see it.

Kampa now brings your glucose onto the same timeline as your tremor, dyskinesia, medication, and meals. If you wear a continuous glucose monitor - any brand, Lingo, Stelo, Libre - the readings flow in automatically through Apple Health. No monitor? A finger-prick reading you enter in Health shows up too.

→ Tremor and glucose, stacked on one shared timeline - scroll them together and watch how a meal, a dose, and your body's response line up.
→ Tap any moment to read the exact values at that minute - tremor, dyskinesia, and glucose, together.
→ It all lives in your Health data, on your device. Kampa reads it; it never leaves your control.

To be clear about what this is and isn't: we're not claiming a glucose reading predicts your tremor. This is about giving you - and your neurologist - an honest, legible picture of how the pieces of a day fit together, so the patterns worth exploring become visible.

It's the same principle behind everything in Kampa: if the data is already in Apple Health, we can show it - no extra logging, no burden.

Kampa is for people living with Parkinson's, the people who care for them, and the researchers working on it. If that's you, I'd value your perspective.

Follow along here, or see more at kampa.health.
