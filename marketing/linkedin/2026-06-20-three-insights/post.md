Most health apps tell you what's true for the average person. Parkinson's doesn't work that way - it shows up differently in everyone who has it.

So we built Kampa to learn *your* patterns, not population averages. This week, it surfaced its first three.

→ How quickly your medication takes effect - and how that shifts across the day. Not every dose behaves the same; some take hold slower, or less completely, than others.

→ When the benefit wears off. Kampa maps how long each dose lasts and when predictable "off" windows tend to cluster - the kind of timing that's hard to feel in the moment but clear in the data.

→ How your walking is trending over the long run. Speed, stability, and symmetry, tracked across years - the direction of travel, not noisy day-to-day readings.

Here's the part that matters most to me: Kampa doesn't guess.

And what you're seeing is real - real data, real output, not a staged mockup. Every insight is computed with statistics and has to clear a real bar before it ever appears. If your data doesn't support a pattern, Kampa stays quiet - no invented trends, no false alarms. We'd rather show you nothing than show you something that isn't real.

The hard part - continuously sensing tremor and movement - happens passively on your Apple Watch, powered by Apple's Movement Disorder API. It's a clinical-grade capability Apple grants access to sparingly, and Kampa is one of the few approved to build on it. No tapping required. And it's all built to bring to your next neurology appointment.

Kampa is for people living with Parkinson's, the people who care for them, and the researchers working on it. If that's you, I'd value your perspective.

Follow along here, or see more at kampa.health.
