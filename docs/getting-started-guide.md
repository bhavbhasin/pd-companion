# Getting Started with Kampa

*A quick guide for your first week. Takes about 5 minutes to set up. After that, Kampa works on its own.*

---

## The one idea

**Kampa works while you do nothing.** You install it, give it permission to read your Apple Health data, and wear your Apple Watch. It quietly notices your tremor, your sleep, how you walk, and your medication timing - and looks for patterns over days and weeks.

You don't have to log anything every day. The watch and your iPhone do the work.

---

## Set it up (3 steps, once)

1. **Install Kampa** from the TestFlight email invitation, then open it.
2. **Allow all three permission screens.** Kampa shows three prompts the first time:
   - **Health data** - tap *Turn On All*, then *Allow*.
   - **Medications** - tap *Allow*.
   - **Motion & Fitness** - tap *Allow*. This one powers tremor tracking - without it, your tremor graph stays empty even though everything else works.

   All three matter. If you tap "Don't Allow" by mistake, you can fix it later:
   - Health: **iPhone Settings → Apps → Health → Data Access & Devices → Kampa**
   - Motion & Fitness: **iPhone Settings → Privacy & Security → Motion & Fitness → Kampa** (make sure it's on)
3. **Set up Kampa on your Apple Watch too.** Tremor tracking runs on the watch, so open the **Kampa app on your Apple Watch once** to start it (install it from the Watch app on your iPhone if it isn't there yet). Then just wear the watch during the day - and overnight if you track sleep. That's it.

> **Watch requirement:** Apple Watch Series 4 or newer, on watchOS 10 or later. Tremor tracking needs the watch on your wrist.
>
> **If your tremor graph stays empty after a day or two,** it's almost always the Motion & Fitness permission (step 2) or the Kampa watch app not being opened (step 3). Check those first.

---

## What happens automatically (no effort)

Once permissions are on, Kampa tracks all of this by itself:

- **Tremor and dyskinesia** - from the Apple Watch
- **Walking / gait** - walking speed, step length, steadiness (from your iPhone in your pocket)
- **Sleep, heart rate, HRV** - from your watch and phone
- **Workouts** - any workout you record with the Apple Watch **Workout app** shows up automatically

You do **not** log any of these by hand.

---

## Optional: sharpen the picture

These are not required. Do them only if you want Kampa to find sharper patterns (for example, "does this medication work slower after lunch?").

- **Food** - log it *inside Kampa*. Tap the **+** button (top-left of the Review screen) → **Food** → type it in plain words ("*5 almonds with tea*") → set the time → **Save**. No counting, no calories.
- **Medications** - set these up in the **Apple Health app** (not Kampa): open Health → *Browse* → *Medications*, add your medications, and log each dose (or use Health's reminders). Kampa reads your dose times automatically and lines them up with your symptoms.
- **Workouts** - just start a workout on your Apple Watch as usual. Nothing to do in Kampa.

---

## Reading your day - the Review screen

The home screen is called **Review**. It shows one day at a time:

- **Top row** - total sleep, HRV, average tremor, dyskinesia for the day.
- **Tremor graph** - your tremor through the day, with small markers for events: 💊 medication, 🍴 food, 🏃 workout.
- **Sleep** - tap to expand and see interruptions.
- **Daily Observations** - gentle notes about *that single day*. These are cautious on purpose.

Use the **‹ ›** arrows near the date to move to other days.

---

## Insights - the patterns over time

Tap the **💡 lightbulb** (top-right of Review) to open **Insights**. This is where Kampa shows patterns it has found across many days, not just today.

Each Insight card shows:

- **A confidence level** - *Watching* (still gathering) → *Emerging* → *Strong*.
- **A plain-language finding** - for example, "*Walking speed is trending down - worth mentioning to your neurologist.*"
- **A "discuss with your neurologist" note** where it matters.

Some insights appear quickly (walking/gait uses years of data your iPhone already has). Tremor-based insights build up over a few weeks of wearing the watch - so the longer you use Kampa, the more it can tell you.

---

## Three things to keep in mind

1. **Kampa is not medical advice.** It helps you *notice* things and bring better information to your doctor.
2. **Never change a medication or dose based on the app.** Always go through your neurologist.
3. **"Nothing found" is still useful.** A calm day with no tremor, or "no clear pattern yet," is real information - not a failure. Kampa is honest about what it does and doesn't see.
